Welcome to my blog!

I am an aspiring techno-psychologist, you may not know what that is, so I would also like to formally invite you to the future! I I am part of a new wave of researchers who will proactively look at the social implications of emerging technology, I would like to focus on bridging the gap between technology and human behavior by encouraging systems of governance and social enterprise.

Today, there are engineers who have created machine learning algorithms which are so complex- to the point where it is very difficult to understand how these algorithms are making decisions. Up until this point, society has been reactive to technology and its benefits as well as it’s consequences- especially the unintended consequences.

It is no surprise that people have become the product with tech giants such as Google and Facebook, using individual data to push consumerism. However, as a result of this we have run into a number of issues. For example, the last presidential election in the United States between Donald Trump and Hillary Clinton, came down to about 100,000 votes. Simultaneously, Facebook has been running a study on a campaign banner, to see if a banner on your Facebook homescreen would influence it’s users to cast a ballot. The results from the Facebook study showed that they were indeed able to influence people- roughly, 100,000 reported voters. High voter turnout, a positive thing in a democratic election system, however, Facebook ads come down to one thing, money. The more money you spend on advertising, the more results you will get. Something as small as a campaign banner now has the power to sway an individual opinion on who to vote for or whether to vote at all. This is one specific example, but we see this across many different platforms. On YouTube, you could be watching a moderately republican video and get eventually do down a rabbit hole and get suggested a KKK video.

Using the internet in today’s day, means that you are putting yourself in a position to be susceptible to confirmation bias. The unintended consequences of technology, specifically artificial intelligence technology has resulted in: surveillance authoritarianism and the surveillance of data, polarization of beliefs, values, people and society, lack of transparency with algorithms and has now brought upon ethical questions for all of us. The information we digest influences us on a personal as well as a societal level, should these complex algorithms- many that we do not understand, have the power to influence us to this extent?

There is an urgent need, to start incorporating behavioral sciences into our efforts to restructure the way our digital technology operates, by looking at business models, structures and policies. My vision is to build artificial intelligence that supports our goals but is constrained by our values, AI that will contribute to our personal and societal well-being. My primary interest lies within the realm of artificial intelligence technology, specifically, on persuasion architectures in the digital world. Unlike in the physical world, online persuasion architectures have no limitations. Implemented by big tech firms to generate profit, persuasion architectures are able to target, infer and understand us. My vision is to not to change a problem we face today but instead to look into emerging technologies and create solutions for the problems we will face in the future. I want to change the world but changing the way in which we have been developing technology, which is perhaps change the world by shifting the way the current world progresses.

Implementation

The way in which I will implement this vision is by producing intentional data-driven policy change at a government level, or changes to practices within organizations. I am going to business school and attaining my M.S in Disruptive Innovation as well in Business Analytics. My curriculum is designed to help me, “develop an understanding of the potential impact of emerging technologies and lead the charge in the next generation of innovation” - Hult International Business School. Through my program, I will be working with other individuals interested in this niche and through collaborations we will come up with projects which will be presented to companies in the Silicon Valley/ Bay Area.
